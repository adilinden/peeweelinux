I could be wrong about this, but I think there's a practical difficulty 
associated with doing that. It's my understanding that the boot image has 
hard coded in it the specific spot on the partition table where the root 
image lives, and so you'd have to redo that for each new device. Otherwise 
we wouldn't need lilo at all. By forcing you to install to an actual 
device, pee wee assures itself that all of the relevant information is 
available to it during the install. There may be a way to set up a 
loopback device with a number of virtual cylinders, heads, etc., 
concurrent with your freind's harware, I haven't explored this option.
