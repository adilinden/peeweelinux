Have you seen Troy Engel's "kernel 2.4 mini-howto"?  It has detailed

Also, for what it's worth, I usually just include my custom kernel (bzImage) 
as a "custom" file, effectively replacing the one created by Peewee.  This is 
certainly not the most *elegant* way to do this (Troy's method is better), 
but it does allow me to control the kernel building process independent of 
Peewee's scripts or compiler dependencies.
